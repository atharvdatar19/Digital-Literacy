# Case Study: UPI Fraud

## Introduction
Unified Payments Interface (UPI) has revolutionized digital payments in India, but it has also led to an increase in sophisticated cyber frauds.

## Scenario
A user receives a call from someone claiming to be a bank representative. The caller informs the user that their KYC is pending and their account will be blocked. They send a link or ask the user to scan a QR code to "verify" their identity or "receive" a refund. The user enters their UPI PIN and funds are instantly deducted from their account.

## Analysis of the Scam
1. **Urgency:** The scammer creates a sense of panic (account blocking).
2. **Deception:** Exploiting the lack of awareness that UPI PIN is **only** required to send money, not to receive it.
3. **Execution:** The fraudster generates a collect request or uses a malicious QR code.

## Prevention & Mitigation
- **Never Share PIN:** UPI PIN should never be shared with anyone.
- **Information:** Remember that receiving money does not require entering a UPI PIN.
- **Verification:** Always verify the identity of the person or merchant before making a payment.
- **Reporting:** Immediately report the fraud to the bank and cybercrime portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call 1930.
- **Awareness:** Stay updated on common fraud tactics.
